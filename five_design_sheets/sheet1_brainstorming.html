Recommended charts (pool)

  • Long-run total enrolments; stacked by sector
  • YoY % change or index (pre-COVID = 100)
  • Lorenz / cumulative curve for source-country concentration
  • Scatter: enrolments vs education exports (years as points)
  • Indexed lines: Australia vs US/UK/CA/NZ (if harmonised)

Interaction ideas

More graph ideas (mix & match)

  • Waterfall or stepped bridge: year-to-year change in total enrolments (pre → COVID trough → recovery).
  • Heatmap: sector × year (cell = % of total or YoY %).
  • Ridgeline / joy plot: enrolment distribution by sector over time (if sub-year data).
  • Dumbbell chart: 2019 vs latest by sector (start–end paired).
  • Connected scatter: trace (enrolment, revenue) path with year labels every 3rd point.
  • Marimekko: sector share × size of total (read with care — label clearly).
  • Streamgraph (alternative to stacked area) for “flows” metaphor — optional, higher visual noise.
  • Calendar heatmap: commencements by month (if monthly data).

Illustration & icon ideas (dashboard chrome)

  • Hero: stylised globe + Australia pin; subtle flight-path arcs for “mobility” (keep abstract, not clichéd stock).
  • COVID band: thin “timeline tape” with icons (plane, border, campus) as watermark, low opacity.
  • Sector pills with tiny glyphs: mortarboard (HE), wrench (VET), speech bubbles (ELICOS), schoolhouse (Schools).
  • Source-country strip: small circular flags + ISO3 labels (accessibility: always show country name).
  • “Stock vs flow” mini diagram: tank + tap sketch beside commence vs enrol chart.
  • Economics panel: ledger-style side ornament (lines only) — not bank notes; suggests “national accounts” seriousness.
  • Ranking panel: podium silhouette with AU on middle tier if rank 1–3; else highlight ring only.
  • Empty-state illustration: dashed map + “select countries” callout for global tab.

Colour & typography

Pencil-first on paper; digitally: charcoal text, off-white ground, one accent (e.g. teal), colorblind-safe sector palette.

Body sans-serif; hand-letter labels on wireframes for warmth.

Why this works / What to avoid

Works: Shows breadth, trade-offs, and traceability to later sheets.

Avoid: Bank/finance framing; implying causation; listing every variable without prioritising a story.
