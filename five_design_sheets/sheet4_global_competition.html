Global Competition — Comparative Dashboard

International Student Mobility and Economic Contribution: Australia’s Position in the Global Education Market

Purpose

Complete third dashboard: position Australia among major study destinations — rankings, international student share, trends, optional map — Australia always visually emphasised.

Intended audience

Readers asking “how does Australia compare globally?” — strategy / internationalisation context.

Main message

Australia is a leading destination with high internationalisation in many metrics; ranks and shares shift over time and depend on definitions.

Recommended charts

  • Horizontal bar chart (rank / volume / share)
  • Bump or slope chart — rank over time
  • Choropleth map placeholder (legend, neutral ocean)

Unique to Alt 4 (do not copy to Sheets 2 or 3): peers, ranks, maps, flows — no commence vs enrol, no revenue scatter.

More graph variants (global / comparison)

  • Beeswarm / strip + jitter: one dot per country, x = students or share, y = jitter only — avoids bar overcrowding.
  • Chord or arc diagram: stylised flows from world regions into Australia (if regional source data).
  • Mirrored bars: AU left of axis, selected peer right — same metric, one row per year or metric.
  • Faceted small-multiple maps: same choropleth repeated for 3–4 milestone years (slider swaps emphasis).

Illustration ideas (geo & peers)

  • Compass rose in corner + subtle graticule (latitude lines) — never use on Sheets 2–3.
  • “Orbit” diagram: AU fixed centre, peer nodes on a ring, line thickness = student link (schematic).
  • Leader lines from highlighted map region to the ranked bar row for the same country.

Interaction ideas

  • Map click ↔ table sync; metric dropdown; pin AU when filtering peers

Colour & typography

Single strong accent for Australia; muted analogous colours for peers; avoid rainbow ramps on the map.

Why this works / What to avoid

Works: Directly answers global positioning; interactions support exploration without losing the AU anchor.

Avoid: Comparing incompatible national definitions without footnotes; cluttering with too many countries.

Draw on sheet: Sketch one Alt-4-only layout (beeswarm, chord, mirrors, or faceted maps); no revenue scatter or commence vs enrol on this sheet.
