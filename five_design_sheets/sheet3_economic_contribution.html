Economic Contribution — Analytical Dashboard

International Student Mobility and Economic Contribution: Australia’s Position in the Global Education Market

Purpose

Complete second dashboard: KPIs, revenue trends, revenue per student, and association between enrolments and education export revenue — with cautious wording.

Intended audience

Analysts, economics-minded supervisors; readers who expect methods and caveats.

Main message

Scale and revenue move together over time (associated with, not “caused by”); COVID years stand out; revenue per student tracks changing intensity.

Recommended charts

  • KPI cards (+ optional sparklines)
  • Time series: export revenue; revenue per student
  • Scatter with regression line; COVID years visually distinct

Unique to Alt 3 (do not copy to Sheets 2 or 4): KPIs, revenue, association, uncertainty — no country ranking, no map.

More graph variants (quant / association)

  • Marginal histograms: rug + small h/v bars on scatter edges (enrolments vs exports).
  • Faceted scatter by period: three panels — pre-COVID | COVID | post — same x/y scales.
  • Rolling correlation: moving window of correlation between growth in enrolments and growth in exports.
  • Bland–Altman–style plot: if two related revenue measures exist — difference vs mean to show agreement.

Illustration ideas (methods & numbers)

  • Light watermark: “association ≠ causation” (once, low opacity).
  • Funnel or ribbon on the regression line = prediction interval (not a map funnel).
  • Unit ladder sketch beside axes ($, students) — reinforces definitions, not geography.

Colour & typography

Muted point cloud; bold only for COVID points; tabular lining for currency; titles use “co-moves”, “associated”, not “drives”.

Why this works / What to avoid

Works: KPIs orient non-specialists; scatter + per-student line covers aggregate and intensity.

Avoid: Causal language; dual y-axis implying false equivalence; mixing fiscal/calendar years without note.

Draw on sheet: Red-box “wording guardrails”; sketch one Alt-3-only graph (e.g. faceted scatter or rolling r) — do not copy Alt 2’s film-strip or Alt 4’s map motifs here.
