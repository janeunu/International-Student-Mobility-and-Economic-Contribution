Australia’s Recovery Story — Linear Narrative Dashboard

International Student Mobility and Economic Contribution: Australia’s Position in the Global Education Market

Purpose

One complete dashboard: a clear time-based story for a general audience — growth, COVID disruption, recovery, sectors, flows vs stock.

Intended audience

Educated public, policy brief readers, markers who value narrative clarity.

Main message

Australia’s international enrolments grew, were hit unevenly by COVID, and recovered by sector; commencements and enrolments tell different parts of the story.

Recommended charts (this dashboard)

  • Total international enrolments — long-run line (COVID band)
  • Sector lines or stacked area — HE, VET, ELICOS, Schools
  • Commencements vs enrolments — aligned x-axis, legend in plain language

Unique to Alt 2 (do not copy to Sheets 3–4): time + story + sectors + commence vs enrol only.

More graph variants (narrative / time)

  • Era ribbon / Gantt strip: pre-COVID | acute disruption | recovery as labelled bands on the same time axis as enrolments.
  • Slopegraph (sectors): left column = 2019 (or peak), right column = latest — one line per sector (HE/VET/ELICOS/Schools).
  • Difference chart: actual enrolments minus a simple pre-COVID trend extrapolation (“gap vs counterfactual”).
  • Cycle / seasonal plot: if monthly data — month-of-year lines with COVID years overlaid in bold.

Illustration ideas (story & chapters)

  • Film-strip frames: three or four sketched panels under the main line = “Act I–III” for growth / shock / recovery.
  • Journey path: single hand-drawn path narrowing through a “bottleneck” band then widening (metaphor only — keep subtle).
  • Chapter tabs drawn as physical folder labels (no globe, no map — those belong to Alt 4).

Colour & typography

Neutral base timeline; distinct sector colours (colorblind-safe); light grey COVID band, not full-bleed red.

Large numbers only for 2–3 milestone years; sentence-case chart titles.

Why this works / What to avoid

Works: Linear path lowers cognitive load; matches assignment themes (growth, COVID, sectors, commence vs enrol).

Avoid: Too many y-axes; burying acronyms; claiming “full recovery” without defining baseline.
